Process

Choose an image from a stock photograph site such as Pexels, Unsplash or Pixabay.

The image should be of a human figure which can be isolated from its environment.

Choose people from a wide variety of backgrounds, ages and races.

Visit a site such as inPixio, Pixlr or HitPaw and upload your chosen image, in order to remove the background.

These three services do not down-scale the image, although HitPaw will output a maximum size of 1,920 pixels wide. Pixlr does not output an image which has a truly transparent background, although this is not an issue for our purposes.

Some clean-up of your image may be required.

Flatten your image and convert it to grayscale.

Convert your image to Bitmap. Depending upon the size of your original image, you may need to play around with the setting here a little. The image used in the example, is 5,637 × 3,763 pixels and used the following settings.

The face of your subject should have a dot density roughly equivalent to the image shown here.

  • Convert your image to greyscale (1:1 size is fine), then convert it again to RGB
  • Resize your image, so it is 2,500 pixels high
  • Select your image using the magic wand, with the tolerance set to 1
  • Inverse your selection
  • Expand your selection by 50 pixels

Give your only layer a name, so it's no longer Background.

Inverse your selection a second time and delete the selected area. This should leave you with your half-tone image with a white background and a thick white border around it.

Download the sunburst shapes file and add it to your Photoshop shapes.

  • Choose a Shape from the new file (or any other appropriate source) and drag it out, so it covers the whole canvas
  • Allow the focal point of the shape to sit behind the figure
  • Add a white background layer
  • Add some colour to your Shape layer
  • Rearrange the layers into a sensible order
