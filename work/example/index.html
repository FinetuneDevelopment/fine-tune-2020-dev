Transforming occupancy rates for 39 nurseries.

The London Early Years Foundation a group of 39 nurseries in central London, were suffering from a convoluted user journey, poor conversion rates and declining applications from parents and prospective employees. Our mission was simple. Increase the number of nursery applications across 39 nurseries, and increase the number of applicants applying for jobs.

The outcome

Driven by the user insights and trends within LEYF’s analytics we were able to deliver a high converting site designed for time-poor parents, who require key answers to their questions in order to book a place. While simultaneously creating a recruitment site focused on helping to solve the problem of distance and travel times to the open opportunity.

The impact

Since launch, we’ve more than doubled conversion rates for parents. Occupancy across 39 nurseries has more than doubled and organic traffic is up 60%. With the rate of applications applying for roles increased by over 30%.

In Conversion Rate Optimisation, the first step to improving your conversion rates is to look at your website data. Your data should tell you where you are losing most of your visitors on the path to becoming customers. What your data probably can’t tell you, however, is why you are losing them. And when you are looking to increase your conversion rates, why is perhaps the most important question.

This post will look at some of the most common reasons so why your visitors aren’t converting into customers. It will also point out what sort of patterns to look for in your web analytics data that would suggest your website might be suffering from these conversion issues.
